Flow Library

Browse and implement ready-to-use PowerAutomate flows. Click a card to see details and instructions.

To add and use any of these flows, go to PowerAutomate Flows , navigate to "My flows" and import the zip package you downloaded. Make sure to select the "Configure" wrench, then "Create new" and establish connections to your own accounts (e.g. SharePoint site, Microsoft Teams, Outlook etc). Without connecting to your own OneDrive, Teams, or whatever app the flow incorporates, the flow will not work.

Message Reply Scheduler

Schedule a message reply to send at a specific time. This workflow lets you schedule repsonses in advance, even when you're not available.

How to Use: Right click the Teams message you want to schedule a reply for and choose the "Schedule a reply" flow. Fill out the date, time, and reply message that you want and submit the form.

Message Follow-Up Reminder

Set up an automated reminder to follow up on specific important messages.

How to Use: Right click on a Teams message and select "Follow up on a message". Set a date, time, and message, then workflows will be triggered to send a reminder email.

Automatic File Backup

Create automatic backups of important documents to SharePoint when files are modified in specific folders.

How to Use: Whenever a document is created in your specified SharePoint folder, a backup will be created in your OneDrive.

Out-of-Office Assistant

Automatically send an Out-of-Office reply when receiving emails in Outlook.

How to Use: When you are out of office, this flow will automatically send an Out-of-Office reply. Make sure to turn this on and off when needed.

Outlook Daily Reminders

Get daily reminders from Outlook with whatever message you want.

How to Use: This is an automatic flow, so it will trigger on its own. But first, after adding the flow, you must go into it and add the email address, subject, and message that you want to send.

Share Emails in Teams

This workflow lets you automatically forward emails based on specific criteria—like recipient address, subject—directly to a designated Teams channel.

How to Use: When adding the flow, go through each field and input your desired information. Make sure th recipient, sender, subject, Team, and Channel are all correct so the right emails get sent to the right place.
